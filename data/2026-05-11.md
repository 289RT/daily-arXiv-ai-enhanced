### [1] [CA-DEL: An Open Multi-Target, Multi-Modal Benchmark for Learning from DNA-Encoded Library Screens](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.07439)
*Mutian He,Hanqun Cao,Cheng Tan,Zijun Gao,Xiaojun Yao,Chunbin Gu,Pheng-Ann Heng*

Main category: q-bio.BM

TL;DR: CA-DEL是一个多维公共基准数据集，用于开发克服DNA编码库（DEL）技术数据噪声影响的机器学习模型，特别关注碳酸酐酶同源异构体的选择性挑战。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 当前缺乏公开基准数据集，以开发能够处理DEL技术间接且噪声信号（如测序读数）的模型。CA-DEL旨在填补这一空白。

Method: CA-DEL基于三种碳酸酐酶同源异构体（CAII、CAIX、CAXII）的筛选数据，集成了ChEMBL中实验测定的结合亲和力（Ki）验证集。

Result: CA-DEL建立了一个从噪声DEL数据训练到高保真生物物理数据测试的评估范式。

Conclusion: CA-DEL为开发稳健的机器学习模型提供了重要资源，特别是在同源异构体的选择性研究方面。

Abstract: The success of machine learning in drug discovery hinges on learning the relationship between a chemical structure and its biological activity. While DNA-Encoded Library (DEL) technology can generate the massive datasets required for this task, its primary signal -- sequencing read counts -- is an indirect and often noisy proxy for true molecular binding affinity. To address the scarcity of public benchmarks for developing robust models that can overcome this data challenge, we introduce CA-DEL, a multi-dimensional public benchmark featuring screens against three homologous carbonic anhydrase isoforms. While recent benchmarks like KinDEL have introduced 3D poses for kinase targets, CA-DEL distinguishes itself by focusing on the selectivity challenge among homologous Carbonic Anhydrase isoforms (CAII, CAIX, CAXII). Unlike benchmarks relying solely on noisy enrichment scores, CA-DEL integrates a rigorous validation set of experimentally determined binding affinities ($K_i$) from ChEMBL, establishing a critical Sim-to-Real evaluation paradigm: training on noisy DEL screens and testing on high-fidelity biophysical data.

### [2] [GoForth: Language Models for RNA Design under Structure, Sequence, and Coding Constraints](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.07608)
*Michael Lindsey*

Main category: q-bio.QM

TL;DR: RNA逆序列设计具有广泛的生物学和工程应用，但计算方法仍有限。GoForth是一个基于条件生成建模的RNA语言模型，结合结构、序列和编码目标，实现了快速高质量的候选序列生成。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: RNA设计需要满足多种约束条件（如折叠、固定碱基、编码限制等），传统方法难以处理这些复杂的混合约束。通过条件生成建模，能够更灵活地生成满足多样化需求的序列。

Method: 提出GoForth模型，将序列先验、折叠采样器和奖励/似然预测分开处理。通过编码器-解码器结构训练，利用观测到的折叠数据而非逆向设计教师模型的输出。

Result: GoForth在逆向折叠基准测试和混合约束任务中表现优异，能够快速生成高质量的候选序列，并提供设计任务的有用语义嵌入和设计性度量。

Conclusion: 条件生成建模为RNA设计提供了一种高效灵活的方法，GoForth的成功展示了其在处理复杂设计约束方面的潜力。

Abstract: RNA inverse sequence design has broad biological and engineering applications, but computational methods for practical design queries remain limited. Such queries may impose several constraints at once, including target folds or motifs, fixed bases, and coding restrictions, while leaving arbitrary sequence and structure in unspecified regions. Because these constraints may permit many acceptable sequences, we study RNA design as a conditional generative modeling problem. The basic object is a conditional law over RNA sequences given a user-specified condition, with full inverse folding as a special case. We introduce GoForth, a forward-trained RNA language model that conditions on structure, sequence, and coding targets. The formulation separates three ingredients that are often entangled in RNA design: a sequence prior, a forward folding sampler, and a reward or likelihood oracle. We train encoder-decoder models on witnessed folds rather than on outputs from an inverse-design teacher and validate our methodology on full inverse-folding benchmarks, as well as tasks involving constraints on structure, sequence, and coding. The resulting models achieve fast and high-quality candidate generation for mixed RNA design specifications. Moreover they furnish useful semantic embeddings of design tasks and a robust learned notion of designability.

### [3] [ProteinJEPA: Latent prediction complements protein language models](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.07554)
*Dan Ofer,Dafna Shahaf,Michal Linial*

Main category: cs.LG

TL;DR: 研究探讨了在蛋白质语言模型中，结合掩码语言建模（MLM）与潜在空间预测（JEPA）的效果，提出了一种新的训练方法，并在多个任务中验证其性能优于纯MLM。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 探讨潜在空间预测是否能与MLM结合，提升蛋白质语言模型的性能。

Method: 提出‘masked-position MLM+JEPA’方法，即在掩码位置预测潜在目标并保留MLM交叉熵损失，同时对不同规模模型进行实验。

Result: 在16个下游任务中，新方法在多数任务中表现优于纯MLM，显示了潜在空间预测的补充作用。

Conclusion: 结合MLM与JEPA的方法在训练中具有竞争力，且在某些情况下优于纯MLM。

Abstract: Protein language models are trained primarily with masked language modeling (MLM), which predicts amino-acid identities at masked positions. We ask whether latent-space prediction can complement these token-level objectives under matched wall-clock budget. Across pretrained and random-init protein sequence encoders at 35--150M parameters, we find that the best protein-JEPA design is not all-position latent prediction but a variant: predicting latent targets only at masked positions, and retaining the MLM cross-entropy. We call this recipe masked-position MLM+JEPA. On a 16-task downstream suite (15 frozen linear probes plus SCOPe-40 zero-shot fold retrieval), under matched wall-clock budgets, this recipe wins more tasks than it loses against MLM-only continuation: 10 wins / 3 losses / 3 ties (hereafter W/L/T) on pretrained ESM2-35M, 11/2/3 on ESM2-150M while results in pretraining from scratch are mixed (6/8/2). Gains are seen for multiple models on 11 of 16 tasks, including stability, \b{eta}β\b{eta}-lactamase fitness, variant effect, intrinsic disorder, remote homology, enzyme classification, and SCOPe-40 fold retrieval. Tasks with more losses than wins are Fluorescence (TAPE) and Peptide-HLA Binding. All-position MLM+JEPA matches MLM-only overall but does not reproduce the masked-position gains. JEPA-only (no MLM) collapses in nearly every experiment. We conclude that JEPA, when combined with MLM, is competitive and can outperform pure MLM in pretraining and continued training, even under matched wall-clock budgets.

</details>
