### [1] [JEDEL: Zero-Shot DNA-Encoded Library Design for Early-Stage Drug Discovery](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.23745)
*Zygimantas Jocys,Zhanxing Zhu,Henriette M. G. Willems,Katayoun Farrahi*

Main category: q-bio.BM

TL;DR: JEDEL框架可从活性配体的三维药效团表示直接生成合成就绪的DNA编码库（DELs），是首个将药效团相互作用模式映射为可扩展合成指令的模型。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 现有生成方法生成的虚拟化合物仍需下游合成规划，而JEDEL直接在可购买构建块和已验证反应空间中操作，确保输出可直接实验实现。

Method: 通过预测药效团几何与分子结构的对应关系，并解码为规模化组合合成路径。

Result: 在18个蛋白靶标上，JEDEL生成的聚焦库在预测结合亲和力、药效团恢复和样本效率上优于随机和多样性基线。

Conclusion: JEDEL推动了从虚拟分子生成到实验可部署库设计的转变。

Abstract: We present JEDEL, a framework for generating synthesis-ready DNA-encoded libraries (DELs) directly from three-dimensional pharmacophore representations of active ligands. JEDEL is the first model to map pharmacophore interaction patterns to actionable, scalable synthesis instructions, enabling the design of targeted libraries comprising potentially millions of molecules. Unlike existing generative approaches that produce virtual compounds requiring downstream synthesis planning, JEDEL operates within the space of purchasable building blocks and validated reactions, ensuring that every output is experimentally realizable by construction. JEDEL learns a predictive alignment between pharmacophore geometry and molecular structure and decodes this into combinatorial synthesis routes at scale. Across 18 protein targets, it generates focused libraries that outperform random and diversity-based baselines in predicted binding affinity, pharmacophore recovery, and sample efficiency, without target-specific retraining. JEDEL enables a shift from virtual molecule generation to experimentally deployable library design.

### [2] [CABS-flex standalone 3: an open command-line platform for protein flexibility simulation, peptide structure modeling, and protein-peptide docking](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.24487)
*Chandran Nithin,Karol Wroblewski,Piotr Szukalo,Ayomide Fasemire,Aleksander Kuriata,Mateusz Kurcinski,Andrzej Kolinski,Sebastian Kmiecik*

Main category: q-bio.BM

TL;DR: CABS-flex standalone 3是一个开源的命令行平台，用于蛋白质灵活性建模、肽结构模拟以及蛋白质-肽对接，支持全原子重建和分析。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 该工具旨在为结构生物信息学领域提供一个高效的蛋白质灵活性模拟和柔性蛋白质-肽对接解决方案。

Method: 基于CABS-flex和CABS-dock生态系统，结合Python 3实现，整合了蛋白质灵活性模拟、线性与环状肽建模、扩展报告与可视化以及基于深度学习的全原子重建功能。

Result: 提供了一个功能丰富的开源工具，支持多种蛋白质模拟和分析任务。

Conclusion: CABS-flex standalone 3为研究人员提供了一个强大且灵活的工具，用于蛋白质结构和相互作用的研究。

Abstract: Summary: CABS-flex standalone 3 is an open command-line platform for fast CABS-based coarse-grained modeling of protein flexibility, peptide structures, and global or information-guided protein-peptide docking, coupled with all-atom reconstruction and analysis. The package builds on the established CABS-flex and CABS-dock ecosystem, widely used in structural bioinformatics for protein flexibility simulations and flexible protein-peptide docking. It provides a Python 3 implementation that brings together previous standalone functionality with recent developments in protein flexibility simulation, linear and cyclic peptide modeling, extended reporting and visualization, and deep-learning-based all-atom reconstruction with cg2all.
  Availability and Implementation: CABS-flex standalone 3 is implemented in Python 3 and is freely available as an open-source command-line package. Documentation is available at https://cabsflex.lcbio.pl. Source code is available at https://github.com/LCBio/CABSflex_standalone.

</details>
