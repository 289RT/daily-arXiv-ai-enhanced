### [1] [Model Reduction of Multicellular Communication Systems via Singular Perturbation: Sender Receiver Systems](https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.11244)
*Taishi Kotsuka,Enoch Yeung*

Main category: eess.SY

TL;DR: 研究了嵌入水凝胶珠中的多细胞发送-接收系统，通过PDE-ODE耦合模型分析扩散信号对异质细胞间相互作用的影响，利用奇异摄动理论将其简化为多智能体网络。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 探索扩散信号在异质细胞间的传递机制，解决PDE-ODE耦合模型的分析与仿真难题。

Method: 使用奇异摄动理论将三维扩散非线性动态模型简化为有限维多智能体网络，并通过球形格林函数推导闭合形式的通信矩阵。

Result: 简化模型与完整动态高度吻合，并支持大规模细胞群体的可扩展仿真。

Conclusion: 提出的简化方法有效解决了PDE-ODE耦合模型的复杂性问题，为多细胞系统研究提供了实用工具。

Abstract: We investigate multicellular sender receiver systems embedded in hydrogel beads, where diffusible signals mediate interactions among heterogeneous cells. Such systems are modeled by PDE ODE couplings that combine three dimensional diffusion with nonlinear intracellular dynamics, making analysis and simulation challenging. We show that the diffusion dynamics converges exponentially to a quasi steady spatial profile and use singular perturbation theory to reduce the model to a finite dimensional multiagent network. A closed form communication matrix derived from the spherical Green's function captures the effective sender receiver coupling. Numerical results show the reduced model closely matches the full dynamics while enabling scalable simulation of large cell populations.

### [2] [Task-Specific Sparse Feature Masks for Molecular Toxicity Prediction with Chemical Language Models](https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.11412)
*Kwun Sy Lee,Jiawei Chen,Fuk Sheng Ford Chung,Tianyu Zhao,Zhenyuan Chen,Debby D. Wang*

Main category: cs.CE

TL;DR: 提出了一种多任务学习框架，结合化学语言模型和任务特定注意力模块，提高分子毒性预测的准确性和可解释性。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 现代药物发现需要可靠的黑盒毒性预测模型，但现有模型的可解释性不足，难以满足高风险安全决策的需求。

Method: 采用多任务学习框架，结合共享化学语言模型和任务特定注意力模块，并通过L1稀疏约束聚焦关键分子片段。

Result: 在ClinTox、SIDER和Tox21数据集上，该框架优于单任务和标准多任务基线，并提供可解释的预测可视化。

Conclusion: 该框架在提高预测性能的同时增强了模型的可解释性，有望推动药物发现中的应用。

Abstract: Reliable in silico molecular toxicity prediction is a cornerstone of modern drug discovery, offering a scalable alternative to experimental screening. However, the black-box nature of state-of-the-art models remains a significant barrier to adoption, as high-stakes safety decisions demand verifiable structural insights alongside predictive performance. To address this, we propose a novel multi-task learning (MTL) framework designed to jointly enhance accuracy and interpretability. Our architecture integrates a shared chemical language model with task-specific attention modules. By imposing an L1 sparsity penalty on these modules, the framework is constrained to focus on a minimal set of salient molecular fragments for each distinct toxicity endpoint. The resulting framework is trained end-to-end and is readily adaptable to various transformer-based backbones. Evaluated on the ClinTox, SIDER, and Tox21 benchmark datasets, our approach consistently outperforms both single-task and standard MTL baselines. Crucially, the sparse attention weights provide chemically intuitive visualizations that reveal the specific fragments influencing predictions, thereby enhancing insight into the model's decision-making process.

</details>
