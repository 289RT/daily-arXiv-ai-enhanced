### [1] [Structural Interpretations of Protein Language Model Representations via Differentiable Graph Partitioning](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.10985)
*Siddhant Dutta,Edward Tan Beng Wai,Soumick Sarker,Pasan Gunawardane,Jagath C. Rajapakse*

Main category: cs.LG

TL;DR: 该论文提出了一种可解释的蛋白质语言模型框架，通过将ESM-2特征投影到蛋白质接触图上，利用SoftBlobGIN进行结构感知的消息传递，显著提升了功能预测的准确性和可解释性。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 蛋白质语言模型（如ESM-2）的特征在功能预测中表现优异，但其隐含的结构和进化信号难以解释。因此，作者希望通过一种可插拔的框架，增强模型的透明度和可审计性。

Method: 提出了一种基于ESM-2的框架，通过SoftBlobGIN（一种轻量级图同构网络）将蛋白质语言模型的特征投影到接触图上，进行结构感知的消息传递，并学习功能性亚结构以支持下游任务。

Result: 在酶分类任务中，该方法达到了92.8%的准确率和0.898的宏F1值；在结合位点检测中，AUROC从0.885提升到0.983，且无需额外训练语言模型。此外，该方法还能自动分组功能性亚结构。

Conclusion: 该框架通过结构感知的消息传递和可解释的亚结构学习，显著提升了蛋白质语言模型的预测性能和透明度，为其提供了一个可审计的结构伴侣。

Abstract: Protein language models such as ESM-2 learn rich residue representations that achieve strong performance on protein function prediction, but their features remain difficult to interpret as structural $\&$ evolutionary signals are encoded in dense latent spaces. We propose a plug-$\&$-play framework that projects ESM-2 representations onto protein contact graphs $\&$ applies $\textbf{SoftBlobGIN}$, a lightweight Graph Isomorphism Network with differentiable Gumbel-softmax substructure pooling, to perform structure-aware message passing $\&$ learn coarse functional substructures for downstream prediction tasks. Across enzyme classification, SoftBlobGIN achieves 92.8\% accuracy $\&$ 0.898 macro-F1. Unlike post hoc analysis of protein language models alone, our method produces directly auditable structural explanations: GNNExplainer recovers biologically meaningful active-site residues, spatially localized functional clusters, $\&$ catalytic contact patterns. On binding-site detection, SoftBlobGIN improves residue AUROC from $0.885$ using an ESM-2 linear probe to $0.983$, indicating that these structural explanations are not recoverable from language-model features alone. Learned blob partitions provide an additional layer of interpretability by automatically grouping residues into functional substructures, with blobs containing annotated active-site residues showing $1.85\times$ higher importance than other blobs ($ρ{=}0.339$, $p{=}0.009$), without any active-site supervision. Our framework requires no retraining of the language model, adds only $\sim$1.1M parameters, $\&$ generalises across ProteinShake tasks, achieving $F_{\max}$ of $0.733$ on Gene Ontology prediction $\&$ AUROC of $0.969$ on binding-site detection. We position this as an interpretable structural companion to protein language models that makes their predictions more transparent $\&$ auditable.

### [2] [Deep Learning for Protein Complex Prediction and Design](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.11189)
*Ziwei Xie*

Main category: cs.LG

TL;DR: 论文通过深度学习方法改进了蛋白质复合物的结构预测和序列设计。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 准确建模和设计蛋白质复合物结构是计算结构生物学的核心问题，对理解细胞功能和开发治疗方法有广泛影响。

Method: 使用深度学习研究两个基础方面：捕捉蛋白质结构层次性的领域特定架构，以及高效搜索蛋白质复合物序列空间的算法，以识别相互作用的同源物。

Result: 改进了复合物结构预测并设计了蛋白质序列。

Conclusion: 深度学习为解决蛋白质复合物结构建模和设计问题提供了有效工具。

Abstract: Accurately modeling and designing protein complex structures is a central problem in computational structural biology, with broad implications for understanding cellular function and developing therapeutics. This thesis investigates two fundamental aspects of this problem using deep learning: domain-specific architectures that capture the hierarchical nature of protein structures, and search algorithms that efficiently navigate the vast sequence spaces of protein complexes to identify interacting homologs for improving complex structure prediction and to design protein sequences.

### [3] [Decomposing the Generalization Gap in PROTAC Activity Prediction: Variance Attribution and the Inter-Laboratory Ceiling](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.11764)
*Thor Klamt,Wolfgang Nejdl,Ming Tang*

Main category: cs.LG

TL;DR: 论文探讨了机器学习预测生化活性时存在的随机分割与留一目标验证之间的泛化差距，并通过蛋白质降解作为实证分析平台，发现实验室间测量方差是主要影响因素。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 揭示随机分割验证与留一目标验证在性能上的差距，并量化其根源，特别是实验室间测量方差的影响。

Method: 使用PROTACs作为测试平台，分析八种已发布架构及ESM-2蛋白质语言模型，并通过超参数优化和分层训练改进性能。

Result: 实验室间测量方差占据主要影响（0.124 AUROC），且LOTO性能稳定在0.67附近；通过分层训练和ADMET特征可将性能提升至0.705。

Conclusion: 研究强调了评估方法对预测性能的影响，并提供了PROTAC-Bench和相关工具以支持未来研究。

Abstract: Machine-learning predictors of biochemical activity often exhibit large random-split-to-leave-one-target-out generalisation gaps that have been documented but not decomposed. We frame this as an evaluation-science question and use targeted protein degradation as the empirical test bed. PROTACs (proteolysis-targeting chimeras) are heterobifunctional small molecules that induce targeted protein degradation, with more than forty candidates currently in clinical trials; published predictors report AUROC of 0.85 to 0.91 under random-split cross-validation, while the leave-one-target-out (LOTO) protocol of Ribes et al. reduces performance to approximately 0.67. Random splits reward within-target interpolation, whereas LOTO measures the novel-target prediction that de-novo design depends on. We decompose this gap and identify inter-laboratory measurement variance as the dominant component, anchored by a within-target cross-laboratory cascade bounding the inter-laboratory contribution at 0.124 AUROC, well above the 0.05 contribution from binarisation-threshold choice. Across eight published architectures and ESM-2 protein language models up to 3B parameters, LOTO AUROC plateaus near 0.67, with a comparable plateau under SMILES-level deduplication; a 21-dimensional 2000-trial hyperparameter optimisation cannot break this ceiling, and the rank-1 single-seed configuration regresses by 0.161 AUROC under multi-seed validation, matching a closed-form selection-bias prediction (Bailey and Lopez de Prado, 2014). Few-shot k=5 stratified per-target retraining combined with ADMET features lifts 65-target LOTO AUROC from 0.668 to 0.7050, and post-hoc Platt scaling recovers raw output to within the 0.05 well-calibrated threshold. We release PROTAC-Bench (10,748 measurements, 173 targets, 65 LOTO folds), the variance-decomposition framework, the per-target calibration protocol, and the evaluation code.

</details>
