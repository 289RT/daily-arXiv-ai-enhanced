### [1] [Towards coevolution-aware ancestral sequence reconstruction](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.27942)
*Alya Zeinaty,Leonardo di Bari,Saverio Rossi,Pierre Barrat-Charlaix,Francesco Zamponi,Martin Weigt*

Main category: q-bio.BM

TL;DR: 该论文提出了一种结合协同进化信息（DCA）的祖先序列重建（ASR）框架，解决了传统方法忽略蛋白质进化中位点间相互作用的问题，并在模拟和实际数据中验证了其有效性。

Motivation: 传统ASR方法假设位点独立进化，忽略了蛋白质进化中的协同作用（epistasis），导致重建的祖先序列可能不准确或功能差。

Method: 论文引入了一个结合标准系统发育推断和直接耦合分析（DCA）的ASR框架，保留了位点间约束关系，并通过模拟进化实验验证方法。

Result: 该方法在β-内酰胺酶和DNA结合域中表现更优，生成的祖先序列既符合系统发育又保留自然蛋白质家族的统计特性。

Conclusion: 该框架填补了单序列重建和无约束后验采样之间的空白，为更真实地反映蛋白质协同进化提供了实用工具。

Abstract: Ancestral sequence reconstruction (ASR) is a powerful approach for studying molecular evolution and the emergence of protein function. Yet most ASR methods assume that sites evolve independently, neglecting the epistatic constraints that shape protein structure, stability, and function. This simplification affects both ancestral inference and its evaluation: maximum-a-posteriori reconstructions may over-concentrate probability into a single over-idealized sequence, whereas independent posterior sampling can generate implausible or poorly functional ancestors. Here, we introduce a coevolution-aware ASR framework that combines standard phylogenetic inference with Direct Coupling Analysis (DCA), thereby preserving site-wise ancestral uncertainty while enforcing residue-residue constraints learned from extant protein families. To benchmark the method, we develop a controlled forward-evolution framework based on a DCA evolutionary sampler, allowing reconstructed ancestors to be compared with known ground-truth sequences generated under realistic epistatic constraints. Applied to beta-lactamases and DNA-binding domains, the approach improves reconstruction when ancestral states are epistatically constrained, and yields ensembles of candidate ancestors that are both phylogenetically consistent and statistically compatible with natural protein families. This framework bridges the gap between single-sequence MAP reconstruction and unconstrained posterior sampling, providing a practical route toward ancestral reconstructions that better reflect the coupled nature of protein evolution.
