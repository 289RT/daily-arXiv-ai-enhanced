### [1] [Integrative modelling of protein-glycan interactions with HADDOCK3](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.17251)
*Victor Reys,Marco Giulini,Alexandre M. J. J. Bonvin*

Main category: q-bio.BM

TL;DR: 该论文提供了一个使用HADDOCK3平台逐步建模蛋白质-聚糖相互作用的协议，通过实验或预测的相互作用约束和柔性优化的方法，适用于类似系统。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 聚糖的结构多样性和柔韧性使其与蛋白质相互作用的建模具有挑战性，而现有方法需要优化。

Method: 使用HADDOCK3平台，结合实验或预测约束，进行柔性优化，并以4-beta-glucopyranose与Humicola grisea Cel12A酶的催化域相互作用为例。

Result: 从非结合结构开始的模型质量尚可，界面-配体RMSD值低于3埃，协议经过多系统优化和验证。

Conclusion: 该协议为蛋白质-聚糖相互作用的整合建模提供了广泛适用的框架。

Abstract: Glycans are structurally diverse and flexible biomolecules that play key roles in many biological processes. Their conformational variability makes the modeling of their interactions with proteins particularly challenging.
  This chapter presents a step-by-step protocol for modeling protein-glycan interactions using HADDOCK3, an integrative modeling platform that supports the inclusion of experimental or predicted interaction restraints and allows for flexible refinement of the solutions. The workflow is illustrated using the interaction between a linear homopolymer glycan, 4-beta-glucopyranose, and the catalytic domain of the Humicola grisea Cel12A enzyme, for which an experimental X-ray structure is available as a reference. Detailed instructions are provided for input structure preparation, restraint definition, docking setup, execution, and result analysis.
  Application of the protocol starting from unbound structures yields models of acceptable to medium quality, with interface-ligand RMSD values below 3 angstroms. Although illustrated on a specific system, the protocol has been optimized and benchmarked on multiple protein-glycan complexes and is broadly applicable to similar systems, providing a framework for integrative modeling of protein-glycan interactions.

### [2] [Atomic Trajectory Modeling with State Space Models for Biomolecular Dynamics](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.17633)
*Liang Shi,Jiarui Lu,Junqi Liu,Chence Shi,Zhi Yang,Jian Tang*

Main category: q-bio.BM

TL;DR: ATMOS是一个基于状态空间模型（SSM）的新型生成框架，用于生成生物分子系统的原子级MD轨迹，填补了传统MD模拟计算成本高与现有生成模型无法建模时序关系的缺口。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 理解生物分子的动态行为对于阐明其功能和促进药物发现至关重要，但传统的MD模拟计算成本高，而现有的生成模型无法建模时序关系或仅适用于单体蛋白质。

Method: ATMOS结合了Pairformer状态转移机制和扩散模型，前者捕捉长程时间依赖性，后者以自回归方式解码轨迹帧，并在PDB晶体结构和大规模MD模拟数据集（如mdCATH和MISATO）上进行训练。

Result: ATMOS在生成蛋白质单体和复杂蛋白质-配体系统的构象轨迹方面达到了最先进的性能。

Conclusion: 通过高效推断原子运动轨迹，ATMOS为生物分子动力学建模奠定了有前景的基础。

Abstract: Understanding the dynamic behavior of biomolecules is fundamental to elucidating biological function and facilitating drug discovery. While Molecular Dynamics (MD) simulations provide a rigorous physical basis for studying these dynamics, they remain computationally expensive for long timescales. Conversely, recent deep generative models accelerate conformation generation but are typically either failing to model temporal relationship or built only for monomeric proteins. To bridge this gap, we introduce ATMOS, a novel generative framework based on State Space Models (SSM) designed to generate atom-level MD trajectories for biomolecular systems. ATMOS integrates a Pairformer-based state transition mechanism to capture long-range temporal dependencies, with a diffusion-based module to decode trajectory frames in an autoregressive manner. ATMOS is trained across crystal structures from PDB and conformation trajectory from large-scale MD simulation datasets including mdCATH and MISATO. We demonstrate that ATMOS achieves state-of-the-art performance in generating conformation trajectories for both protein monomers and complex protein-ligand systems. By enabling efficient inference of atomic trajectory of motions, this work establishes a promising foundation for modeling biomolecular dynamics.

</details>

### [3] [Non-perturbative Bacterial Identification Directly from Solid Agar Plates Using Raman](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.16957)
*Jeong Hee Kim,Jia Dong,Marissa Morales,Loza Tadesse*

Main category: q-bio.QM

TL;DR: 该研究提出了一种无需打开琼脂平板即可进行拉曼光谱细菌识别的方法，结合DFT计算和机器学习，实现了高精度分类。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 解决拉曼光谱在微生物识别中因额外制备步骤而受限的问题，实现直接从未打开的琼脂平板上进行细菌识别。

Method: 利用拉曼光谱结合密度泛函理论（DFT）计算和机器学习分析，从未打开的琼脂平板上识别细菌，并通过拉曼映射验证。

Result: 方法在多种培养基和基底材料条件下，分类准确率超过97.7%，优于传统方法。

Conclusion: 该方法具有高鲁棒性，可用于细菌生长、生物膜形成和抗药性发展的实时无损监测。

Abstract: Raman spectroscopy is a promising tool for microbial identification, yet its implementation in microbiology and clinical workflow is still restricted due to the accompanying additional preparation required to focus on microbial signals. Here, we demonstrate Raman-based bacterial identification directly from unopened, inverted agar plates, the same conditions used during incubation. Our approach enabled identification with single gene-level sensitivity using two Escherichia coli variants, differing only in green fluorescent protein (GFP) expression, across diverse media and substrate material conditions, despite the interrogation path traversing 3-4 mm thick background material. We integrated traditional density functional theory (DFT)-based material computation with machine learning analysis, achieving over 97.7% classification accuracy, surpassing the performance of standard measurements from opened plates by 10.8% higher mean accuracy and 0.76% less variance. We further demonstrated Raman mapping-based colony identification via Raman peaks characteristic to GFPmut3 chromophore structure generated by DFT. Our approach is robust to changes in algorithms or substrate materials and promises real-time, non-perturbative monitoring of bacterial growth, biofilm formation, and antimicrobial resistance development.

</details>
